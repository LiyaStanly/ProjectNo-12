Experience the Khasi Culture

Delectable Khasi Cuisine

Khasi cuisine is known for its simplicity and use of local ingredients. The food is generally healthy, with minimal oil. A must-try dish is **Jadoh**, a fragrant rice dish cooked with meat and spices. Other popular items include **Doh-Khlieh**, a pork salad, and **Tungrymbai**, a fermented soybean curry.

Vibrant Festivals and Dances

Festivals are a significant part of Khasi life. The **Nongkrem Dance**, a five-day festival, is a thanksgiving ceremony to the deity for a good harvest. The **Shad Suk Mynsiem**, or "Dance of the Joyful Heart," is another popular folk dance that celebrates the traditional faith of the Khasi people.

Local Handicrafts and Music

The Khasis are skilled artisans, known for their cane and bamboo products. You can find intricately woven baskets, furniture, and other unique items. Music is also integral to the culture, with folk instruments like the **Duitara** (a four-stringed instrument) and the **Tangmuri** (a traditional flute) being commonly used in celebrations and rituals.
